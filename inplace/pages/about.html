Companies and brands are increasingly called upon to play a role and use their influence in social issues and with urban spaces becoming more crowded fast-changing and pressed to develop than ever before. We believe that companies need bespoke and high quality information in order to navigate these landscapes and make decisions that fit and benefit their brands as well as the communities they serve.

Squad..

Stef Lai – Founder and Lead Consultant

A Hong Kong native now living in New York and previously London, Stef has a passion for observing and exploring different facets of urban life. She has experience working in market research and consulting in the USA and the UK, and in founding In Place is combining this with her expertise gained from a Masters in Urban Sociology from Goldsmiths, University of London. Stef is committed to technology-forward and creative, interactive methods of research and presentation. Stef’s other pursuits include cycling, climbing and more recently, pinball.

Vivian Song – Lead Consultant

Vivian is a versatile researcher and moderator based between Shanghai and New York City. She obtained an MA in Urban Sociology from Goldsmiths College, London. In her 10 years experience in conducting research in cultural and brand world, blending experience in research journalism, urban studies, ethnography and cultural content production, Vivian has developed unique experience and methods to help clients understand culture and consumption habits within the Chinese market and develop actionable strategies for innovation in service and products.

Kelly Neuner – UX Designer and Strategist

Kelly Neuner is an experience designer, strategist, and futurist based in Seattle, Washington (previously based in New York). She’s passionate about holistic design that balanced individual needs with broader impacts, turning insights into to tangible plans of action, and leveraging technology for creative storytelling.

Over the past eight years, she has consulted for clients from startups to Fortune 500 brands, led integrated experience strategies for Fast Company, and managed social strategy and communications for a large non-profit. She studied at The New School & Parsons, where she developed her own interdisciplinary curriculum around design, business, and communications.

She is a passionate competitive cyclist committed to equitable access.

Liliah Guertin (they/them) – UX Designer and Strategist

This Minneapolis resident with a travelers heart seeks out to create inclusive spaces for all people. Emerging into the design world in 2018 with a background in education, communications strategy and aiding in community organizations, they became attracted to advocacy based design. Primarily focused on market and user research they enjoy solving complex problems by focusing on a company's touch-point strategy to create a symbiotic ecosystem for your products roll-out. Lilah’s main interest is finding innovative avenues your product can positively impact the humans that are important to you. On most days Lilah will most likely be playing guitar or riding bicycles in other cities.
